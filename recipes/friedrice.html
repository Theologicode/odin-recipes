Fried Rice

Description

Fried rice is a genuine Chinese dish, originating approximately 589 AD. It's coming about was economical, in that the dish allowed the reuse of left over rice and other staple ingredients in a dish that was also tasty. It's earliest documentation is in the Jiangsu province of China, one of Emperor Yang's favorite dishes. (Courtesy of: Google Gemini. Accessed 9/20/24. Prompt:'History of fried rice.' Generated using https://Google.com).

Ingredients

  • 1 medium sized collander full of Rice
  • 1 can of Peas
  • 1 Egg
  • Wok oil
  • 1 cup finely chopped carrots
  • 1 lb. steak, chicken, pork, shrimp, or fish cut into common sense sized bits
  • Seasoning when dish is finished: sweet and sour or teriaky sauce
  • season rice to taste with soy sauce prior to using teriaky sauce.

Steps

  1. Place rice in a medium metal collander inside a medium pot half filled with water to boiling. Boiling water will give off steam, use steam to cook rice for approximately 45 minutes or until tender.
  2. add single egg to wok and stir continually so yolk and white mix
  3. Heat wok oil until it fizzles lightly in pan when a drop of water is added
  4. add completed rice to pan/wok as well as some of the strained peas and carrots. Stir continually until rice is lightly fried.
  5. Add soy sauce to taste once rice is finished.
  6. Add 1lb. cooked meat. User Sweet & Sour or Orange Sauce, Teriyaki Sauce to eat dish.
